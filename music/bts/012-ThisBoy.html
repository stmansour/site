Behind The Scenes #12
This Boy
  (link to video)

This is a very special video for me for a couple of reasons. One reason, of course, is that it is a Beatles song and any Beatles song I record will always be special. I think their music is the finest and most timeless of any music ever written. I want to do justice to their music and I try to be faithful to the vibe and feel they gave to their music. I respect and appreciate people who change the style or arrangement of any cover song to make it their own. But I've never heard an arrangement of a Beatles song that I like better than the original version. Maybe that's because theirs was the first version I heard, or maybe it's that the minds that created the song also created the arrangement with the same magic, or maybe it's something else that I can't express. Whatever the reason, that's why I stay faithful to their style and arrangement.

But an even more important reason this song is special is that I did it with my life-long musical partner Matt Kramer. Matt and I have been writing music together since we were teenagers. We've written hundreds of songs together but we haven't really recorded much together. Matt is a great singer and I knew it would be fun and nostalgic for us to do a Beatles song. What brought us together as teenagers was our mutual love of writing our own music. And as part of that process, we found that we found that we had a shared love of the Beatles music. We would often jam together and play Beatles songs. This Boy is a song that we played a lot, doing two part harmony (I sang John's part, Matt sang Paul's) and wished we had someone to do the third part (George's). Just before the COVID lockdown, Matt made a trip to my house. We would be busy with a bunch of things, but on a whim I asked him to pick a Beatles song and we would record it, something we had never done before. So, I laid down some initial tracks, the vocal part I always sang, and the vocal part we were always missing. When Matt arrived we laid down his vocal. We should have captured the video while Matt was singing his part, but somehow, we forgot and Matt shot his video from his house while under lockdown. And I did the same. And finally, a little more than a year later, I slammed it all together in this video.

One interesting bit of trivia about this song is that the original version the Beatles played had a guitar solo where the bridge is now. George Martin did not like the guitar solo and they worked out that bridge with John's searing vocal.
